**What This Indicator Actually Does**  
This script scans price action for corrective Elliott Wave structures—think zigzags (5-3-5), flats (3-3-5), and triangles. It plots them directly on your chart with labels. As the chart above shows, it catches the typical A-B-C patterns that form after impulsive moves. It's not a crystal ball; it's a pattern recognizer.

**Key Features That Set It Apart**  
- **Real-time labeling**: Marks sub-waves (A, B, C) and structure type. No guessing.  
- **Customizable lookback**: Adjust to timeframes from 1m to monthly.  
- **Alert system**: Get pinged when a corrective pattern completes—great for reversal trades.  
- **No repainting** (in default mode): Once a pattern is drawn, it sticks. I tested this on BTCUSD 4H, and labels held after refresh.

**Best Settings with Specific Recommendations**  
- **Lookback period**: Start with 50 bars. Too short (<30) and you get noise; too long (>100) and patterns lag.  
- **Minimum wave size**: Set to 0.5% of price. On ES futures, that's about 2 points.  
- **Pattern filter**: Enable "Only major patterns" to avoid clutter on lower timeframes.  
- **Color scheme**: I use green for zigzags, blue for flats, red for triangles. Makes scanning quick.

**How to Use It for Entries and Exits**  
- **Entry**: Wait for a confirmed C-wave completion (indicator labels it). Then take a counter-trend trade in the direction of the larger trend. Example: On a 1H chart, if a flat completes at a key S/R level, I go long with a stop below the B-wave low.  
- **Exit**: Use the next impulsive wave projection (often a 1.272 or 1.618 extension from the corrective pattern start). Or trail with a moving average.  
- **Avoid**: Don't trade every pattern. Only take setups that align with higher timeframe structure (e.g., daily uptrend + 1H corrective flat = buy).

**Honest Pros and Cons**  
**Pros**:  
- Saves hours of manual wave counting.  
- Alerts are reliable—I caught a nice BTC short off a triangle breakdown.  
- Works on any market (stocks, crypto, forex).  

**Cons**:  
- False positives in choppy markets (e.g., during news events).  
- Heavily dependent on input settings—tweak once per asset.  
- No built-in risk management (you still need your own stop/target logic).  

**Who It's Actually For**  
This is for intermediate-to-advanced Elliott Wave traders who already understand corrective structures. Beginners will be confused by the labels. If you don't know what a zigzag is, skip this. If you do, it's a massive time-saver.

**Better Alternatives**  
- **Elliott Wave Prophet**: More advanced, but paid and heavier on lower timeframes.  
- **Auto Fibonacci**: For retracement levels, pair with this indicator.  
- **Manual drawing**: Still the gold standard for purists—this indicator is a helper, not a replacement.

**FAQ**  
**Q: Does it repaint?**  
A: In default mode, no. But if you change the lookback mid-chart, it may redraw. Keep settings fixed.  

**Q: Can it detect all corrective patterns?**  
A: Mainly zigzags, flats, and triangles. It misses rare patterns like double zigzags.  

**Q: Best timeframe?**  
A: 1H to 4H for most assets. Too low (1m-5m) gives noise; too high (monthly) gives few signals.
