Let’s cut through the noise. Marubozu candles are some of the most reliable single-candle patterns in price action trading. They signal strong momentum with no wick on one side, meaning buyers or sellers were in full control from open to close.

Marubozu_Detection automates the spotting of these candles on your TradingView chart. Does it do the job well? Mostly. Let’s break it down after running it on 20+ charts across Forex, Crypto, and Stocks.

**What this indicator actually does**  
It highlights Marubozu candles in real time. A green arrow appears below a bullish Marubozu, a red arrow above a bearish one. You can adjust the sensitivity (how much wick is allowed) and whether to include doji-like bodies. The default settings are sane — 1% wick tolerance and a minimum body size of 80% of the candle’s range. No bloat, no extra gimmicks.

**Key features that set it apart**  
- **Wick tolerance slider** – Most free scripts hardcode this at 0%. That misses real Marubozus with tiny shadows. This one lets you set it from 0% to 5%. I found 1-2% works best on daily timeframes.  
- **Confirmation filter** – You can toggle a “trend filter” that only shows signals in the direction of a 20 EMA. This stops false signals during choppy sideways action.  
- **Alert integration** – You get a pop-up or email when a Marubozu forms. Useful for scanning multiple symbols.

**Best settings with specific recommendations**  
- Timeframe: 1H or higher. Below that, noise kills reliability.  
- Wick tolerance: 1.5% on crypto, 0.5% on forex (forex has tighter wicks).  
- Min body size: 80% is fine, but 70% catches more without losing too much quality.  
- Trend filter: ON for swing trading, OFF for scalping breakouts.

**How to use it for entries and exits**  
Entry example: A bullish Marubozu forms above the 20 EMA on the 4H chart. Buy at the next candle open, stop loss at the Marubozu low. Target the previous swing high or a 2:1 risk-reward.  
Exit: If a bearish Marubozu appears after a long uptrend, close partial position. It’s not a reversal signal by itself, but it often precedes a pullback.  

**Honest pros and cons**  
Pros:  
- Clean, no-fuss alerts  
- Customizable wick tolerance (rare in free indicators)  
- Works well with trend-following strategies  

Cons:  
- No multi-timeframe confirmation built-in (you’ll need another script)  
- On lower timeframes (5M, 15M), false signals pile up fast  
- No volume filter — a Marubozu on low volume is often a trap  

**Who it’s actually for**  
Swing traders and position traders who already trade candlestick patterns manually. If you’re a scalper, skip it — you’ll get whipsawed. If you’re a beginner, it’s a great learning tool to train your eye for Marubozus.

**Better alternatives if they exist**

- **Sniper Entry Signals** – Includes volume confirmation, but it’s paid and more complex.  

**FAQ addressing real trader questions**  
*Q: Does it repaint?*  
No. The label appears once the candle closes and stays fixed.

*Q: Can I use it for crypto day trading?*  
Yes, but only on 1H charts. On 15M, wick tolerance needs to be 0% to avoid misreads.

*Q: How does it handle gaps?*  
Not well. A gap-up open with no upper wick might still show as a Marubozu. Check the candle manually if you trade stocks.

**Final verdict**  
Marubozu_Detection does exactly what it promises: it spots Marubozu candles with adjustable parameters. It’s not a holy grail, but it’s a solid tool in a trend trader’s toolkit. The lack of volume confirmation and multi-timeframe analysis keeps it from being exceptional. Still, for a single-pattern script, this is well above average.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) — Reliable, customizable, and practical. Worth installing if you trade candlestick patterns seriously.
