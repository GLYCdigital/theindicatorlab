# Alligator (Bill Williams) Review

The Alligator indicator, developed by Bill Williams, is a trend-following tool that uses three smoothed moving averages (the Jaw, Teeth, and Lips) to visualize market trends and their strength. The indicator is designed to help traders stay in sync with the market by entering trades when the lines are aligned and exiting when they cross. It is particularly useful for filtering out noise and identifying when a trend is beginning or ending, making it a staple for traders who prefer to follow rather than predict price movements.\n\nThis indicator is part of the Williams Trading System and works best in trending markets. When the three lines are intertwined (the alligator sleeps), the market is range-bound. When they separate and fan out (the alligator wakes up), a strong trend is underway. Traders often use the Alligator in conjunction with other Bill Williams tools like the Awesome Oscillator or Fractals to confirm signals and improve accuracy.\n\nThe Alligator is versatile and can be applied to any timeframe, but its default settings (Jaw: 13-period SMMA, Teeth: 8-period SMMA, Lips: 5-period SMMA) are optimized for daily charts. For scalping, shorter periods can be used, while swing traders may prefer longer settings to capture larger moves. Understanding how to use the Alligator effectively requires patience and discipline, as it is not a leading indicator but rather a confirmation tool.

## Key Features

- Three smoothed moving averages (Jaw, Teeth, Lips) with built-in displacement for future-looking signals
- Color-coded lines to easily identify trend direction and alignment
- Customizable periods and displacements for different trading styles
- Visual representation of trend strength through line separation (fanning out)
- Compatible with most trading platforms like MetaTrader 4, TradingView, and ThinkorSwim

## How to Use Alligator (Bill Williams)

1. Default settings: Jaw (13,8), Teeth (8,5), Lips (5,3) for daily charts – ideal for swing trading
1. Scalping: Use shorter periods like Jaw (9,5), Teeth (6,3), Lips (3,1) on 5-15 minute charts for faster signals
1. Swing trading: Increase periods to Jaw (21,13), Teeth (13,8), Lips (8,5) on 4-hour to daily charts to filter out minor fluctuations
1. Entry: Enter long when Lips cross above Teeth and Jaw, and all lines are sloping upward; exit when lines cross or intertwine
