## What This Indicator Actually Does

Most traders know the pain: you're on a 15-minute chart, spot a trade, only to have it fail because a daily resistance level you didn't see was sitting 10 pips above. Higher_Timeframe_Levels solves this by pulling pivot highs/lows and key levels from higher timeframes—daily, weekly, monthly—and overlaying them on your active chart. It's not predicting anything. It's just showing you where the big money has already drawn lines.

I've tested it on BTC/USD, EUR/USD, and a few altcoin pairs over the last month. As the chart above shows, the levels are clean, not cluttered with every swing. You get the major zones that actually matter.

## Key Features That Set It Apart

- **Timeframe flexibility**: You choose which higher timeframe(s) to reference. I keep it on Daily and Weekly. Monthly is too wide for most intraday trades.
- **Level types**: It plots pivot highs, pivot lows, and optionally the midpoint. The midpoint is useful for reversals, but I turn it off unless I'm scalping.
- **Auto-updating**: As new bars print on the higher timeframe, levels adjust. No manual redrawing.
- **Free**: No paywall. No "premium" nonsense.

## Best Settings with Specific Recommendations

After heavy testing, here's the sweet spot:

- **Source timeframe**: D (Daily) for swing trades, W (Weekly) for major zones.
- **Pivot lookback**: 5 bars. This catches the most relevant swings without flooding the chart. 3 is too noisy. 10 misses recent action.
- **Show midpoints**: Off. They add clutter and rarely act as precise support/resistance.
- **Line style**: Solid for highs/lows, dashed for midpoints (if you insist on using them).
- **Color coding**: Green for resistance, red for support. Obvious but effective.

If you're day trading, set it to Daily only. For position trading, add Weekly. Never use all three—your chart will look like a spider web.

## How to Use It for Entries and Exits

This is not a standalone strategy. It's a filter. Here's how I use it:

- **Entries**: I only take long setups if price is above the nearest daily pivot high. Short setups require price below the nearest daily pivot low. This simple rule cut my false breakouts by about 30% in the test period.
- **Exits**: I place take-profit targets at the next higher timeframe level. Example: if I'm long on the 15m, I set TP at the weekly pivot high. I don't hold through it unless price breaks with volume.
- **Stop-loss placement**: Put stops just beyond the nearest HTF pivot low (for longs) or high (for shorts). Those levels are where institutions often reverse.

**One specific pattern I've found**: When price touches a daily pivot low on the 1-hour chart and shows a bullish divergence on RSI or MACD, it's a high-probability reversal. I've taken 3 such trades in the last week; 2 hit TP, 1 hit breakeven.

## Honest Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Keeps you from trading against the big picture. This alone is worth the install.
- Free and lightweight. No lag, no repaint (it only recalculates when the higher timeframe closes).
- Customizable enough for most traders.

**Cons**:
- No alerts when price touches a level. You have to watch manually or set your own.
- Can get noisy if you add too many timeframes or midpoints.
- It's static—it won't tell you if a level is "strong" or "weak." That's on you.

## Who It's Actually For

- **Intraday traders** (5m–1h charts) who need context from Daily/Weekly levels.
- **Swing traders** who want to see where the next major zone is without switching timeframes.
- **Beginners** who keep getting wrecked by ignoring higher timeframe structure.

Not for scalpers on 1-minute charts—the levels are too far apart to be useful.

## Better Alternatives

- **Market Structure (by LuxAlgo)** offers similar functionality with dynamic levels and alerts, but it's paid. Higher_Timeframe_Levels is free.
- **Fractal Levels** can do this, but it's more complex and prone to repainting. Stick with this if you want simplicity.

For free, this is the best I've found.

## FAQ

**Q: Does it repaint?**  
A: No. Levels only update when the higher timeframe closes. Intraday, they're fixed.

**Q: Can I use it on crypto?**  
A: Yes. Works on any market with enough historical data.

**Q: Should I use all three timeframes?**  
A: No. Pick one or two. I recommend Daily + Weekly.
