If you’ve ever stared at a chart trying to manually draw Fibonacci extensions for a WXY correction pattern, you know the pain. This indicator handles that grunt work. It automatically identifies W-X-Y structures (common in Elliott Wave theory) and plots potential target zones based on the pattern’s geometry. No guessing, no ruler tool. Just clean, objective levels.

I’ve been testing this on MACD charts (as the developer recommends) and it’s surprisingly effective for catching continuation moves after complex corrections. Here’s what you need to know before installing it.

**What it actually does**

The indicator scans price action for three-swing patterns where waves W and Y move in the same direction, connected by an X wave (counter-trend). Once identified, it draws horizontal target lines using the length of wave W projected from the end of wave Y. You get two key levels: a primary target (typically 100% of W) and an extended target (161.8% or 127.2%, depending on your settings). It’s a pure trend-following tool — not a reversal predictor.

**Key features that stand out**

First, the auto-detection is clean. Unlike some pattern tools that flood your chart with noise, this one only draws targets when a valid WXY structure completes. Second, you can toggle between Fibonacci ratios in the settings — I prefer 161.8% for extended targets on higher timeframes. Third, the labels are customizable: change their color, size, and position so they don’t overlap with your price action.

**Best settings I’ve tested**

After about 50 trades across BTCUSD, EURUSD, and AAPL, here’s what works:

- **Timeframe:** 1H or 4H. Lower timeframes (15m) produce too many false patterns.
- **Wave sensitivity:** Default (medium). The “strict” setting misses valid patterns; “loose” catches too many fakeouts.
- **Target ratio:** 161.8% for extended targets. 100% is fine for quick scalps, but the real meat is in the extension.
- **Show labels:** On. But keep them small — large labels clutter the chart on MACD.

**How to actually use it**

Entry logic is straightforward: wait for the indicator to plot a target after the Y wave completes. Don’t enter immediately — let price confirm with a breakout of the X wave’s high/low. As shown in the chart above, the best entries come after a clean break of that X wave level, with volume supporting the move.

Set your stop loss just beyond the end of wave Y. Take partial profits at the primary target (100% of W), then trail the rest to the extended target. On a 4H BTCUSD trade last week, this setup gave me a 2.3R win. Not earth-shattering, but consistent.

**Pros & Cons**

*Pros:*  
- Saves hours of manual pattern drawing  
- Works well with strong trends (add a 200 EMA for extra confirmation)  
- Clear, objective levels — no interpretation needed  

*Cons:*  
- Useless in ranging markets (it will draw targets that never hit)  
- Doesn’t account for volume or momentum — you need to filter trades yourself  
- The “auto” part can lag; sometimes the pattern completes, but price reverses before the target is plotted  

**Who it’s for**

This is for traders who already understand WXY patterns and want to automate the target calculation. If you trade Elliott Wave or harmonic patterns and use trend-following strategies, it’s a time-saver. Beginners will struggle because the indicator doesn’t explain *why* a pattern is valid — it just draws lines.

**Alternatives worth considering**

- **Auto Fibonacci Retracements** (free) — gives you standard fib levels without pattern detection. Better for simple trends.  
- **Patternz** (paid) — detects more pattern types (ABCD, Gartley, etc.) but is noisier.  
- **Manual drawing** — if you only trade one or two patterns, you don’t need this tool.

**FAQ**

**Does it work on crypto?** Yes, but only on higher timeframes (4H+). Crypto’s volatility triggers false patterns on 1H.

**Can I use it with MACD?** The developer shows it on MACD charts, but I’ve tested it on regular candlestick charts and it works fine. MACD just helps confirm momentum.

**Why don’t targets always get hit?** The indicator projects targets based on wave geometry, not market psychology. Always use a stop loss.

**Final Verdict**

W_X_Y_Pattern_Auto_Targets_Jpt is a solid 4/5. It does one thing well — automate WXY target drawing — and saves time for experienced traders. It’s not a magic bullet, and it won’t make you money in choppy markets. But if you trade trends and understand the pattern, it’s a reliable assistant. Just don’t expect it to predict the future. No indicator does.
