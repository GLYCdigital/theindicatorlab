I’ve seen a dozen session-based indicators, and most are just fluff—pretty lines that don’t mean much. New_York_Session_Levels is different. It marks the real open, high, low, and close of the New York session (9:30 AM ET to 4:00 PM ET) and projects those levels forward. Simple, but effective.

## What It Actually Does

This indicator draws four horizontal lines on your chart: the NY session open, high, low, and close. These levels remain static until the next NY session, giving you a clear framework for intraday trading. As the chart above shows, price often reacts to these levels—bouncing off the open, breaking through highs, or respecting the low.

No repainting. No predictive algorithms. Just clean, historical data plotted for you.

## Key Features That Matter

- **Session-specific focus**: Not generic daily levels—these are purely NY session data. Perfect for US traders.
- **Customizable time zone**: You can set it to your local time, so the levels appear exactly when you need them.
- **Clean visuals**: Lines are thin, non-intrusive, and color-coded. You can adjust opacity and line style.
- **Alerts on breakouts**: Built-in alert conditions for price crossing any of the four levels. Saves you from staring at charts all day.

## Best Settings I’ve Tested

I ran this on BTC/USD and EUR/USD 5-minute charts for two weeks. Here’s what worked:

- **Line style**: Dashed for high/low, solid for open/close. Makes visual scanning faster.
- **Opacity**: 50% for high/low, 70% for open/close. Enough to see, not enough to clutter.
- **Time zone**: Set to UTC-4 (NY local). Do not trust auto-detect—manually set it.
- **Extend levels**: Turn this ON. It projects levels through the next session, which is where the real value is.

## How to Actually Trade With It

This isn’t a buy/sell signal generator. It’s a framework. Here’s how I use it:

- **Open as pivot**: If price stays above the NY open after the first 30 minutes, I look for long entries on pullbacks to that level. Below open? Short bias.
- **High/low breakouts**: A clean break of the NY high with volume? I enter long with a stop at the open. If price rejects the high, I short back to the open.
- **Close as magnet**: Late in the session, price often drifts back toward the NY close. Scalpers can play that mean reversion.

**Risk management**: The NY low is your hard stop for longs, the NY high for shorts. This is non-negotiable.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**:
- Zero repainting—what you see is what you trade.
- No lag. This is pure data, not a moving average.
- Works on any time frame below 1-hour (lower TFs are better).
- Completely free on TradingView.

**Cons**:
- Only useful for US session traders. Asians and Europeans will get less value.
- No multi-session comparison (e.g., comparing NY open to London open).
- Lines can feel static if you’re used to dynamic indicators.

## Who It’s For

- Day traders who focus on the NY open (stocks, forex, futures).
- Traders who hate repainting indicators.
- Anyone who wants a clean, no-nonsense level framework.

**Not for**: Swing traders, overnight hold strategies, or anyone who trades purely on indicators.

## Alternatives

- **Session Map** – Shows all three major sessions (Asia, London, NY) with full OHLC. Better for global traders.
- **Daily Open/Close Levels** – Simpler, just the daily open and close. Good if you want fewer lines.
- **FTD (First Trading Day) Levels** – More complex, uses first hour data. Better for breakout traders who want tighter levels.

## FAQ

**Q: Does this repaint?**  
A: No. The levels are fixed once the NY session closes.

**Q: Can I use it on crypto?**  
A: Yes, but the NY session doesn’t have the same significance for crypto. Works best on equities and forex pairs involving USD.

**Q: What time frame should I use?**  
A: 5-minute or 15-minute for intraday. 1-hour if you’re trading the session breakouts.

**Q: Does it work with pre-market data?**  
A: No. It starts at 9:30 AM ET sharp.

New_York_Session_Levels does one thing and does it well. It’s not flashy, but it’s reliable. If you trade the US session, it’s a must-have in your toolkit. If you don’t, skip it. Four stars because it’s niche—but within that niche, it’s nearly perfect.
