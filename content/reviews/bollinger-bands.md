Let’s cut the fluff. Bollinger Bands isn’t some secret weapon. It’s a volatility-based tool that’s been around since the 1980s, and John Bollinger himself would probably roll his eyes at half the YouTube strategies claiming to "trade the bands." I’ve used this on everything from Bitcoin to FX majors for years. Here’s what actually works.

## What This Indicator Actually Does

Bollinger Bands plots three lines on your chart: a 20-period simple moving average (SMA) in the middle, and two outer bands set at a standard deviation (usually 2) above and below that SMA. The bands expand when volatility increases and contract when it drops. That’s it. No magic. No hidden signals.

The key insight: price tends to stay within the bands about 95% of the time when using 2 standard deviations. When it breaks out, something statistically unusual is happening.

## Best Settings I Actually Use

The default (20, 2) is solid for daily and 4-hour charts. But here’s what I tweak depending on the asset:

- **For crypto (BTC, ETH):** Use 20, 2.5. Crypto whipsaws more. That extra half deviation filters out noise.
- **For forex (EURUSD, GBPJPY):** Stick with 20, 2. Forex respects the bands better than any other market I’ve tested.
- **For stocks (SPY, AAPL):** Try 20, 1.8 during earnings season. Bands tighten pre-release, then explode.

Change the SMA length to 50 if you’re swing trading weekly charts. The bands become smoother, but you’ll get fewer signals.

## How I Actually Use It for Entries and Exits

Forget "buy the bottom band, sell the top band." That’s a one-way ticket to getting stopped out. Here’s what I do:

**Entry (Trend Continuation):**
- Wait for the bands to squeeze (low volatility period). This is the "Bollinger Band Squeeze."
- When price breaks above the upper band with a strong candle close *and* the bands start expanding again, I go long.
- Stop loss? 1 ATR below the lower band.

**Exit (Mean Reversion):**
- If price is hugging the upper band for 3+ candles, I look for a bearish divergence on RSI or MACD. That’s my signal to take profit or short.
- Same logic in reverse for the lower band.

**False Breakout Filter:**
- This is my favorite trick. If price touches the upper band but the next candle closes inside the bands again, ignore it. The market is rejecting that level. Only trade the break if the close stays *outside* the band.

## Honest Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Works on every timeframe and market I’ve tested.
- The squeeze is a reliable volatility predictor.
- The bands are self-adjusting—no need to redraw support/resistance constantly.

**Cons:**
- Terrible for ranging markets. Bands become useless when price just meanders inside them.
- Mean reversion trades on intraday charts (1m, 5m) are noisy. You’ll get faked out constantly.
- It’s lagging by nature. The bands react to past price, not future moves.

## Who This Indicator Is Actually For

- **Swing traders:** Your bread and butter. Daily charts with 20,2 are gold.
- **Volatility traders:** The squeeze setup is perfect for you.
- **Beginners:** It’s simple to understand and hard to misuse if you follow rules.

**Not for:** Scalpers on 1-minute charts. You’ll get chopped to pieces.

## Better Alternatives

- **Keltner Channels:** Similar but uses ATR instead of standard deviation. Better for gap-prone markets like crypto.
- **Donchian Channels:** Plots the highest high and lowest low over a period. Better for breakout traders who want to see actual price extremes.

But honestly? Bollinger Bands is still the king for volatility-based analysis because it’s mathematically sound and universally recognized.

## FAQ – Real Questions I Get

**Q: Should I use 1 or 2 standard deviations?**
A: 2 is the standard for a reason. 1 gives you too many false breaks. Only use 1 if you’re trading ultra-tight ranges with a very short stop.

**Q: Do Bollinger Bands work on crypto?**
A: Yes, but adjust the deviation to 2.5. Crypto loves to spike hard. The extra buffer saves you from fakeouts.

**Q: What timeframes are best?**
A: Daily for swing trades. 1-hour for intraday. Avoid anything under 15 minutes unless you’re scalping with a very tight stop.

**Q: Can I use Bollinger Bands alone?**
A: No. Pair it with volume or RSI. Alone, it’s just a volatility band. You need confirmation.

## Final Verdict

Bollinger Bands is a 5-star tool because it’s simple, effective, and adapts to market conditions automatically. It won’t make you money by itself—no indicator does—but it’s the closest thing to a "universal" volatility gauge that exists. If you don’t have it on your chart, you’re trading blind to volatility.
