I’ve been burned by harmonic pattern indicators before—half of them repaint like a cheap watercolor, and the other half give you so many false signals you might as well flip a coin. So when I saw *Deep_Crab* pop up in the 07 category, I was skeptical. But after a month of forward testing on BTC, EUR/USD, and a handful of altcoins, I can say this one actually earns its place on your chart.

Let’s cut the fluff.

## What Deep_Crab Actually Does

This indicator automatically detects **Crab harmonic patterns**—the deep retracement variant of the Gartley family. You know the setup: XA, AB, BC, and CD legs with specific Fibonacci ratios (typically 0.886 for XA retracement and 1.618 or 2.618 extensions on BC). Deep_Crab plots the pattern directly on your chart, marks the potential reversal zone (PRZ), and draws the legs with clean lines.

No AI hype. No “machine learning” nonsense. Just harmonic geometry that works—if you know how to read it.

## Key Features That Actually Matter

- **Clean visual output** – Unlike most harmonic tools that look like a spider web, Deep_Crab keeps the lines minimal. The PRZ is highlighted with a shaded zone, and the pattern label includes the expected reversal direction.
- **Real-time scanning** – It scans across all visible timeframes automatically. You don’t need to manually search for patterns.
- **Alerts** – You can set an alert when a new Crab pattern forms. This is clutch for catching setups while you’re away from the screen.
- **No repaint on confirmed patterns** – This was my biggest concern. I tested it on 1,000+ historical bars. Once a pattern is fully formed (all legs complete), the lines stay put. Early-stage patterns *will* repaint as new bars form—that’s standard for any harmonic indicator.

## Best Settings (What I Actually Use)

After testing multiple configurations, here’s what works:

- **Pattern sensitivity**: Default (0.85) – lower values catch more patterns but increase false signals. I keep it at 0.85 for a good balance.
- **Minimum leg length**: 20 bars – anything shorter looks like noise on higher timeframes.
- **Show invalid patterns**: OFF – you don’t need visual clutter from broken patterns.
- **PRZ extension**: 0.886 (Crab) – this is the signature ratio. Stick with it.

For timeframes, it shines on **1H to 4H**. Lower than 15M gets too noisy. Higher than daily, patterns take weeks to form—fine if you’re a swing trader, but most users will find 1H-4H optimal.

## How I Use It for Entries and Exits

Look at the chart above—you’ll see a completed Crab on BTC/USD in the 4H timeframe. Here’s my playbook:

1. **Wait for PRZ touch** – The pattern must fully form and price must enter the shaded PRZ zone. No early entries.
2. **Check confluence** – I look for additional confirmation: RSI divergence, a support/resistance level, or a candlestick reversal pattern (pin bar or engulfing).
3. **Entry** – Place a limit order at the 0.886 Fibonacci level within the PRZ. Stop loss goes 1-2% below the PRZ low.
4. **Targets** – First target is the 0.382 retracement of CD leg. Second target is the 0.618. Third target is the start of the pattern (point X). On the BTC setup in the chart, that gave me a 3:1 risk-reward on the first target.

**Important:** Never enter blind on the pattern alone. Harmonic patterns are probability tools, not guarantees. I’ve seen Deep_Crab print perfect patterns that failed immediately because the broader trend was too strong.

## Cons (Real Talk)

- **Only detects Crab patterns** – If you want Bat, Gartley, or Butterfly, you need a separate indicator. This is a single-pattern tool.
- **No multi-timeframe confirmation** – You have to check higher/lower timeframes manually. Some competitors show pattern strength across timeframes.
- **Default settings misfire on low volume pairs** – On thinly traded altcoins, I had to increase minimum leg length to 30 bars to filter false patterns.

## Who Is This For?

**Swing traders** and **position traders** who use harmonic analysis. If you already understand Fibonacci ratios and PRZ zones, Deep_Crab saves you hours of manual pattern hunting. 

**Not for**: Scalpers or day traders on 1-minute charts. The patterns form too slowly for that style.
