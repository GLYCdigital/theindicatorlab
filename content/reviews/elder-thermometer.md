Let’s cut the BS. The **Elder_Thermometer** isn’t a magic bullet, but it’s one of the few momentum tools that actually works if you understand what it’s measuring. I’ve traded with it on everything from Bitcoin to crude oil, and here’s the real story.

### What This Indicator Actually Does

The Elder_Thermometer is a momentum oscillator that measures the "temperature" of the market on a scale of 0 to 100. It’s based on Alexander Elder’s concept of tracking the strength of buying and selling pressure. Unlike RSI, which smooths price changes, this one uses raw price change and volume to calculate the reading. The result? A cleaner, less lagging reflection of market intensity.

You’ll see a single line bouncing between 0 and 100. In the chart above, the line spikes into the red zone (above 80) when buying pressure peaks, and dips into the blue zone (below 20) when selling pressure climaxes. It’s not a trend-follower—it’s a **contrarian tool** for spotting exhaustion.

### Key Features That Set It Apart

- **Volume-weighted calculation** – Most oscillators ignore volume. This one doesn’t. That’s a big deal for spotting real climaxes vs. noise.
- **Adjustable lookback period** – Default is 13 (like many of Elder’s tools). I’ll get to why you should change it below.
- **Overbought/oversold thresholds** – The 80/20 lines are hardcoded but work well. No need to tweak them.
- **Zero-line cross** – The line can go negative in a crash, which is rare but useful for panic bottoms.

### Best Settings (Tested)

- **Timeframe**: Daily or 4H. Anything lower and the noise kills the signal. On 1-minute charts, it’s useless.
- **Period**: 13 is fine, but I prefer **21**. It smooths out the spikes without losing the core signal. For crypto, try 34.
- **Thresholds**: Keep 80/20. Don’t change them—they’re calibrated for this calculation.

Don’t use the default 13 on volatile assets like crypto. You’ll get whipsawed.

### How to Use It for Entries and Exits

**Long entry**: Wait for the line to hit 20 or below, then look for a bullish divergence (price makes a lower low, thermometer makes a higher low). The chart above shows exactly this setup—price new low, thermometer refuses to confirm. That’s your trigger.

**Exit**: When the line crosses above 80, take partial profits. Don’t wait for it to come back down—it can stay extended in strong trends.

**Short entry**: Same logic reversed. Above 80, wait for bearish divergence.

**Warning**: Don’t trade the first touch of 80 or 20. Wait for a divergence or a clear reversal candle. The thermometer can stay in overbought/oversold for multiple bars in a strong trend.

### Honest Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Volume integration makes it more reliable than RSI or Stochastic.
- Works beautifully on daily charts with clear divergence patterns.
- Simple visual—no clutter.

**Cons**:
- Useless in strong trends without divergence confirmation.
- Lag is still present (it’s an oscillator, not a leading indicator).
- The 0-100 scale can feel arbitrary until you see a false signal.

### Who It’s Actually For

- **Swing traders** on daily or 4H charts.
- **Contrarians** who like catching exhaustion moves.
- **Volume-aware traders** who want more than price-only oscillators.

It’s **not** for scalpers, breakout traders, or anyone trading on 5-minute charts.

### Better Alternatives

- **RSI with volume (VWAP-based)** – More widely supported, but noisier.
- **Elder’s Force Index** – Similar concept, but smoothed and easier to spot divergences.
- **Chaikin Money Flow** – If you want pure volume-weighted momentum.

If you already use RSI, stick with it. The Elder_Thermometer is a decent upgrade but not a game-changer.

### FAQ

**Q: Why does it show negative values sometimes?**  
A: That’s a crash signal. It happens when selling pressure is extreme. Don’t trade it—just note it for context.

**Q: Can I use it on forex?**  
A: Yes, but volume is tick-based on forex. It’s less reliable. Stick to futures or stocks.

**Q: What timeframe is best?**  
A: Daily. 4H is okay. Never below 1H.

### Final Verdict

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)**

The Elder_Thermometer is a solid, no-nonsense momentum oscillator that earns its keep if you respect its limits. It’s not revolutionary, but it’s reliable for catching exhaustion in trending markets. Take off half a star because it’s not great in choppy sideways action and the default period needs adjustment for most assets.

If you’re a swing trader who already uses divergence, add this to your toolkit. If you’re a trend follower, skip it.
