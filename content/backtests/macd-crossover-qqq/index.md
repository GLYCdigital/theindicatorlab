## How the MACD Crossover Works

The macd crossover is one of the most widely-used signals in trading. It's simple, mechanical, and removes emotional decision-making from your trading by generating clear entry and exit signals based on the MACD indicator.

On QQQ, the MACD acts as a trend filter. It won't catch every exact bottom, but it reliably captures the meat of major moves while keeping you out of chop.

## 5-Year Performance on QQQ

Over five years (2021-05-26 → 2026-05-22), the MACD Crossover delivered a **+12.9% total return** on QQQ, compounding at **2.5% annually**. 

At **0.08**, the MACD earned less than one units of return for every unit of risk taken. For context, anything above 1.0 is considered good; above 1.5 is excellent.

The catch? A **24.1% max drawdown**. At some point during this 5-year run, you'd have been down nearly 24% from peak equity. That's significant territory for most traders. If you can't handle that kind of drawdown, this isn't for you — or you need to size down.

## Does the MACD Crossover Work on QQQ?

The macd crossover struggled to generate meaningful edge on QQQ over this 5-year period. With a **0.08 Sharpe ratio** and **1.17 profit factor**, the risk-adjusted returns are below what most systematic traders would consider acceptable.

This doesn't mean the MACD is useless — it may work better on different assets, timeframes, or when combined with other filters. But on QQQ with default parameters over 5 years, it didn't produce a compelling standalone edge.

Our take: skip it on QQQ unless you're combining it with other confirmations or using it on different timeframes.
