## How the MACD Crossover Works

The macd crossover is one of the most widely-used signals in trading. It's simple, mechanical, and removes emotional decision-making from your trading by generating clear entry and exit signals based on the MACD indicator.

On BTC, the MACD acts as a trend filter. It won't catch every exact bottom, but it reliably captures the meat of major moves while keeping you out of chop.

## 5-Year Performance on BTC

Over five years (2021-05-26 → 2026-05-23), the MACD Crossover delivered a **+80.7% total return** on BTC-USD, compounding at **12.6% annually**. 

At **0.39**, the MACD earned less than one units of return for every unit of risk taken. For context, anything above 1.0 is considered good; above 1.5 is excellent.

The catch? A **44.4% max drawdown**. At some point during this 5-year run, you'd have been down nearly 44% from peak equity. That's stomach-churning territory for most traders. If you can't handle that kind of drawdown, this isn't for you — or you need to size down.

## Does the MACD Crossover Work on BTC?

The macd crossover shows moderate edge on BTC — **0.39 Sharpe, 1.30 profit factor** over 68 trades. It's not a home run, but it's also not random.

The 33.8% win rate means you'll be wrong more than half the time. The profit factor above 1.0 means your wins are bigger than your losses, which is what keeps you profitable.

Our take: usable as a confirmation tool, but not as a standalone system on BTC. Combine with trend filters or volume confirmation for better results.
