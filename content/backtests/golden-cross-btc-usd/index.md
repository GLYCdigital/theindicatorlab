## How the Golden Cross Works

The golden cross is one of the most widely-used signals in trading. It's simple, mechanical, and removes emotional decision-making from your trading by generating clear entry and exit signals based on the SMA indicator.

On BTC, the SMA acts as a trend filter. It won't catch every exact bottom, but it reliably captures the meat of major moves while keeping you out of chop.

## 5-Year Performance on BTC

Over five years (2021-07-14 → 2026-07-12), the Golden Cross delivered a **+81.8% total return** on BTC-USD, compounding at **12.7% annually**. 

At **0.37**, the SMA earned less than one units of return for every unit of risk taken. For context, anything above 1.0 is considered good; above 1.5 is excellent.

The catch? A **36.0% max drawdown**. At some point during this 5-year run, you'd have been down nearly 36% from peak equity. That's stomach-churning territory for most traders. If you can't handle that kind of drawdown, this isn't for you — or you need to size down.

## Does the Golden Cross Work on BTC?

The golden cross shows moderate edge on BTC — **0.37 Sharpe, 3.54 profit factor** over 4 trades. It's not a home run, but it's also not random.

The 75.0% win rate means you'll be wrong more than half the time. The profit factor above 1.0 means your wins are bigger than your losses, which is what keeps you profitable.

Our take: usable as a confirmation tool, but not as a standalone system on BTC. Combine with trend filters or volume confirmation for better results.
