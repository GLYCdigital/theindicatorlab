## How the SuperTrend + ATR Trailing Stop Works

The supertrend + atr trailing stop is one of the most widely-used signals in trading. It's simple, mechanical, and removes emotional decision-making from your trading by generating clear entry and exit signals based on the SuperTrend indicator.

On QQQ, the SuperTrend acts as a trend filter. It won't catch every exact bottom, but it reliably captures the meat of major moves while keeping you out of chop.

## 5-Year Performance on QQQ

Over five years (2021-07-14 → 2026-07-10), the SuperTrend + ATR Trailing Stop delivered a **-24.5% total return** on QQQ, compounding at **-5.5% annually**. 

At **-0.31**, the SuperTrend earned less than one units of return for every unit of risk taken. For context, anything above 1.0 is considered good; above 1.5 is excellent.

The catch? A **35.9% max drawdown**. At some point during this 5-year run, you'd have been down nearly 36% from peak equity. That's stomach-churning territory for most traders. If you can't handle that kind of drawdown, this isn't for you — or you need to size down.

## Does the SuperTrend + ATR Trailing Stop Work on QQQ?

The supertrend + atr trailing stop struggled to generate meaningful edge on QQQ over this 5-year period. With a **-0.31 Sharpe ratio** and **0.87 profit factor**, the risk-adjusted returns are below what most systematic traders would consider acceptable.

This doesn't mean the SuperTrend is useless — it may work better on different assets, timeframes, or when combined with other filters. But on QQQ with default parameters over 5 years, it didn't produce a compelling standalone edge.

Our take: skip it on QQQ unless you're combining it with other confirmations or using it on different timeframes.
