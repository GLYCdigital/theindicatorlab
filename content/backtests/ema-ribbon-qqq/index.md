## How the EMA Ribbon Works

The ema ribbon is one of the most widely-used signals in trading. It's simple, mechanical, and removes emotional decision-making from your trading by generating clear entry and exit signals based on the EMA indicator.

On QQQ, the EMA acts as a trend filter. It won't catch every exact bottom, but it reliably captures the meat of major moves while keeping you out of chop.

## 5-Year Performance on QQQ

Over five years (2021-05-26 → 2026-05-22), the EMA Ribbon delivered a **+81.5% total return** on QQQ, compounding at **12.7% annually**. 

At **0.67**, the EMA earned less than one units of return for every unit of risk taken. For context, anything above 1.0 is considered good; above 1.5 is excellent.

The catch? A **19.0% max drawdown**. At some point during this 5-year run, you'd have been down nearly 19% from peak equity. That's significant territory for most traders. If you can't handle that kind of drawdown, this isn't for you — or you need to size down.

## Does the EMA Ribbon Work on QQQ?

The ema ribbon shows moderate edge on QQQ — **0.67 Sharpe, 2.85 profit factor** over 16 trades. It's not a home run, but it's also not random.

The 37.5% win rate means you'll be wrong more than half the time. The profit factor above 1.0 means your wins are bigger than your losses, which is what keeps you profitable.

Our take: usable as a confirmation tool, but not as a standalone system on QQQ. Combine with trend filters or volume confirmation for better results.
